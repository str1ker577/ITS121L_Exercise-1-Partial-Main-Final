The BMW M550i xDrive boasts a powerful 4.4-liter TwinPower Turbo V8 engine, delivering an impressive 530 horsepower. This enables the sedan to accelerate from 0 to 60 mph in just 3.8 seconds, showcasing its sporty and dynamic capabilities.

Equipped with BMW's xDrive system, the M550i ensures optimal traction and stability. This intelligent all-wheel-drive technology constantly monitors road conditions, distributing power between the front and rear axles to maximize performance and handling in various driving situations.

The M550i features an M Sport suspension that enhances driving dynamics. Lowered and tuned for sportier handling, this suspension system provides a responsive and agile driving experience without compromising comfort during daily commutes. This sedan offers an Adaptive M Suspension Professional option, allowing drivers to tailor the ride to their preferences. Dynamic Damper Control adjusts shock absorber firmness, adapting to changing road conditions and driving styles for an optimal balance of comfort and performance.

Delivering exceptional stopping power, the M Sport brakes on the M550i are designed for high-performance driving. The large brake discs and multi-piston calipers provide consistent and precise braking, enhancing safety and control.

Audi

The Audi RS7 is the RS6's slightly less practical, but prettier sibling. Where the RS6 has enough space in the trunk for a bunch of suitcases, the RS7 is sleek and angular with a full-width lightbar on the back.

Other than the different rear styling, the two high-performance Audis are identical. Both have the same 4.0-liter twin-turbo V8 as the Porsche Panamera, which sends 600 hp to all four wheels via an 8-speed automatic.

Mercedes

The Mercedes-Benz E63S AMG is the BMW M5's biggest rival in terms of performance, luxury, and outright speed. The E63 features the 4.0-liter twin-turbo V8 used in many other AMG applications but is in this case boosted to 604 hp and 630 lb-ft of torque.

The M5 and E63 are often reviewed together due to their similarities, but the M5 is simply the better driver's car. Sure, the E63 may have more impressive technology and a throatier exhaust noise, but the M5 still beats it in a straight line and around racetracks.

Cadilac

Some cars are big-bodied and some are thrilling. The BMW M5 is both, with a body based on the regular 5-series and a heart-and-lung transplant courtesy of the brand's M performance division. Under the hood lives a spectacular 600-hp twin-turbo V-8 bolted to an eight-speed automatic transmission that powers all four wheels. An optional Competition package turns up the heat with 17 extra horsepower, a more soulful exhaust, stiffer suspension, and Competition badging and trim. That version rocketed to 60 mph in 2.8 seconds in our testing. Built to handle the rigors of mountain hairpins, blasts on the autobahn, and everyday life the M5 offers a premium experience with a penchant for fireworks. Unlike its closest competitor, the Cadillac CT5-V Blackwing, the Bimmer's stealthy packaging isn't offset by a thunderous exhaust but its impressive comfort and refinement make it among the best in the premium sports sedan segment.

Porsche

The Porsche Panamera Turbo S is a fantastic sports sedan from the German manufacturer of the best sports car on the planet. Originally designed to be the 4-door equivalent to the brilliant 911, the Panamera has become a model line on its own.

The top-spec Panamera Turbo S is a worthy competitor to the BMW M5 but focused more on the luxury aspect of the sports sedan. The Turbo S e-Hybrid makes a lot more power but is weighed down with batteries. Still, the Panamera is a great choice with an almost unparalleled build quality.
